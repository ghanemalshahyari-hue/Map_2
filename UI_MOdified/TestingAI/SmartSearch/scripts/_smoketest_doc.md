FM 5-0 Smoketest — MDMP basics

The Military Decisionmaking Process (MDMP) is an iterative planning
methodology used by Army staffs. It has seven steps: receipt of
mission, mission analysis, course of action development, course of
action analysis, course of action comparison, course of action
approval, and orders production.

The 1/3-2/3 rule of time management directs commanders to allocate
one-third of available planning time to themselves and their staff,
and two-thirds of available planning time to subordinate units so
that subordinates can plan and rehearse before execution.

The chief of staff integrates the work of the staff sections during
the operations process. The G-3 / S-3 (operations officer) is
responsible for planning, preparing, executing, and assessing
operations on behalf of the commander.
